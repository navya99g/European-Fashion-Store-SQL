\# Key Business Insights from SQL Analysis



This document summarizes the major insights derived from the European Fashion Store SQL analysis.



---



\## 1. Revenue Concentration

\- A small percentage of products contribute to a large share of total revenue.

\- Pareto analysis revealed that approximately 20% of products account for nearly 80% of category-level revenue.



---



\## 2. High-Value Customers

\- Revenue-based segmentation classified customers into High, Medium, and Low value groups.

\- High-value customers contribute disproportionately to total revenue and should be prioritized for retention strategies.



---



\## 3. Product Profitability

\- Not all high-selling products are profitable.

\- Several products generate negative profit due to high costs or excessive discounts, indicating a need for pricing optimization.



---



\## 4. Campaign \& Channel Performance

\- Campaign-based sales outperform generic sales channels in terms of revenue efficiency.

\- Certain channel-campaign combinations consistently generate higher revenue per item sold.



---



\## 5. Customer Behavior Trends

\- Returning customers contribute more stable and consistent revenue over time.

\- Customers purchasing across multiple categories tend to have higher lifetime value.



---



\## 6. Time-Series Growth Patterns

\- Month-over-month revenue trends show seasonal variations.

\- A subset of customers demonstrates strong growth in purchase quantities, indicating potential upsell opportunities.



---



\## 7. Discount Impact

\- Discounts contribute significantly to revenue variation across categories.

\- Categories with higher discounts do not always translate to higher profitability.



---



\## Business Recommendation

\- Focus marketing efforts on high-value customers and profitable product categories.

\- Optimize pricing and discount strategies to reduce loss-making products.

\- Leverage campaign and channel insights to improve marketing ROI.
